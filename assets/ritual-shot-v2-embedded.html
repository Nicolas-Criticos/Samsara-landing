The Lost Practice

Before supplements,
before science —
there was the olive.

For thousands of years across the Mediterranean, the first act of the morning was simple and sacred: a spoonful of olive oil, taken on an empty stomach, sometimes with lemon, sometimes with herbs gathered at dawn.

It wasn't a trend. It wasn't marketed. It was how people lived — an unspoken agreement between body and earth. Farmers, healers, grandmothers — they all knew. The olive tree gives more than oil. It gives a way of being.

"Somewhere between industrialisation and convenience, we forgot. We replaced ritual with routine, and nourishment with speed."

The Samsara Ritual Health Shot is not a new invention. It is a remembering. Three ingredients — extra virgin olive oil, fresh lemon, and sceletium — cold-pressed and raw, combined in a single morning dose that grounds the body, sharpens the mind, and reconnects you to something older than any wellness brand.

Made on a small farm in the Swartberg Karoo, from olive trees being brought back to life after years of neglect. Every shot carries that story — of patience, rehabilitation, and the quiet belief that what the land offers, when honoured, is more than enough.

This is not simply a product.
It is an invitation back to conscious living.
