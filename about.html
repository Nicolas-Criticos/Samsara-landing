How it started

A grove abandoned.
A vision reclaimed.

Thirteen years ago, 16,000 olive trees were planted across forty irrigation blocks on VrischGewagt Boutique Olive Farm in the Swartberg Karoo — a bold vision for abundance in one of South Africa's most spectacular landscapes. At peak, the farm produced thousands of litres of cold-pressed extra virgin olive oil per year.

Then came years of drought, infrastructure failure, and operational strain. The irrigation network collapsed. Block by block, the trees fell into decline. Today, less than 1% bear fruit — not because the land gave up, but because the system did.

Nicolas Criticos — electrical engineer, farmer, and steward of this land — is bringing them back. Not with shortcuts, but with soil science, restored infrastructure, and patient, methodical care. Every bottle of Samsara olive oil directly funds this work.

What rehabilitation actually means

It takes more than water.
It takes a system.

Rehabilitating neglected olive trees is a multi-year, multi-layer process. It's not just about turning the taps back on — the trees have been stressed for years. Soil biology has collapsed, root systems are compromised, canopies are overgrown and unproductive. Real recovery requires a coordinated approach across several fronts simultaneously.

💧
Irrigation Restoration
Repairing and re-pressurising 40+ year-old drip systems, replacing burst mainlines, clearing blockages — block by block, tree by tree.
🌱
Soil Rehabilitation
Applying compost, biochar, and organic amendments to rebuild microbial life. Karoo soils are ancient and mineral-rich — they just need the biology back.
✂️
Structural Pruning
Removing years of deadwood and cross-branching. Opening the canopy to light. Redirecting the tree's energy into productive growth rather than survival.
🧪
Foliar & Nutrition Programs
Targeted foliar sprays and slow-release fertilisation programs timed to the tree's phenological cycle — from dormancy to flowering to fruit set.
📋
Block-by-Block Tracking
Every block is monitored individually. Health ratings, irrigation status, and intervention logs are tracked in real time to inform decisions season by season.
⏳
Time & Patience
Even with everything done right, stressed olive trees take 2–4 seasons to fully recover. This is not a quick fix — it's a commitment to the long arc.
The 2026 Season Plan
🌿
Recovery Phase (Apr – May)
Irrigation restoration on Blocks 1–3. Foliar support. Post-harvest tree stabilisation. Begin soil amendment program on active blocks.
🍂
Dormancy Prep (May – Jun)
Heavy structural pruning. Compost application. Weed suppression. Prepare Blocks 4–6 for activation.
❄️
Dormancy Work (Jun – Aug)
Deep soil work. Biochar integration. Infrastructure repair on next-phase blocks. No irrigation pressure — trees resting.
🌸
Spring Activation (Aug – Sep)
Restart irrigation on all active blocks. Nutrient push. Foliar spray program. Monitor flower bud development on Blocks 1–3.
